The FF91 is Faraday Future's first flagship consumer car. For eight months I led a team of six product designers and collaborated with a team of in-house UX designers and 3D artists to create functional and intelligent UI space within this luxury vehicle.

Below you can find some details on how we approached this task. The nature of the produced work is still confidential, but please reach out for more information.

The ask

Our main deliverable was to develop a scalable design system, allowing to build consistent in-car UI. The screens that we tackled included everything from center information display and door panels to heads up display.


As a design leader, I had to introduce tools and workflows that would help my team complete the work of building this new design system and also ensure that Faraday Future team will be able to maintain & build upon it in the long-term.


We used Sketch libraries to build up a “single source of truth” for every symbol and Abstract to collaborate on and version our screen layouts. Finally, Zeplin was used for a hand off.


Although we were presented with extensive UX documentation on the day one of the project, we improved upon many user flows as we were designing our way through the system. In addition to that, we were heavily involved in creating some of the core in-car experiences, such as voice control and navigation systems.

Specifics of my role

  • Set up and helped roll out the new scalable design system for the FF91 UI

  • Facilitated a number of workshops to align key stakeholders on the common design vision (from a look and feel for the 3D product renders to icons to UI screens)

  • Developed many detailed creative briefs in order to effectively establish, drive and articulate design vision to my team and client

  • Briefed on-site and remote designers on their daily tasks

  • Provided feedback and guidance on my team’s work

  • Communicated across Faraday’s internal teams, including design, ergonomics, writing and engineering

  • Presented design work-in-progress to obtain feedback from key stakeholders

Takeaway

Creating the most connected car on the market means a lot of UX/UI design considerations. Being embedded in the client’s design team and working alongside their developers meant we could really understand the complex car ecosystem and create a design system that serves product needs. My biggest learning from this process is that eight months is definitely not enough time to fully realize a design system and get it rolled out organization-wide. Yet, our work resulted into a system that set a solid foundation for the internal design teams to continue working independently.
