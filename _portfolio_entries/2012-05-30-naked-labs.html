The Naked Labs 3D body scanner tracks the user’s shape, measurements, body fat %, and weight to give insights into how the user lifestyle affects their health. The team at Naked Labs and Edenspiekermann got together to create their new brand and website experience that would feel as premium and thought-out as their product.

Challenge

When we started our partnership with the Naked Labs team, their old website didn’t do a good job in showcasing company’s vision, was lacking engaging storytelling around the product, and didn’t provide a robust customer support experience. The client wanted a new website to help them nurture their product pre-order signups and ultimately build a long term community around their brand.

Research

As a basis for the brand concept and website development, we started with 3 day immersion phase at the Naked Labs offices.


Our first step was to fully understand Naked Labs business, the environment in which they operated and identify opportunities for their new brand. Besides conducting a focused desk research and holding a series of stakeholder interviews, one of my first tasks was to leverage the user information of the company’s 25-person beta test group to develop archetypes representative of the priority audiences the new website needed to engage with.

By asking a simple question - what role Naked Labs scanner plays in your life? - I analyzed the behavioral patterns that the beta test group users shared and elevated their insights to archetypes.


What I arrived at was seven archetypes that guided our decision making around features, functionality and content for the new Naked website:

The curious newbie

Looking to lose weight, build strength and accept their body. Needs motivation and guidance when it comes to diet and exercise.

The goal focused

Has specific fitness goals and is determined to achieve them. Insecurities about their body  drives them to better results.

The regiment focused

Wants to get back in shape. Needs discipline to stay on track and requires frequent feedback on their progress to stay motivated.

The data enthusiast

Wants to visualize, measure and track their fitness. Loves pushing their limits and has thirst for fitness and diet knowledge.

The physical appearance focused

Driven millennial who cares about physical appearance. Passionate about fitness and enjoys competing. Brand ambassador in making.

The holistic health focused

Aware of their body limitations but willing to do anything to improve their health and emotional wellbeing.

The brand ambassador

Believes in Naked. Very knowledgeable about fitness and health. Maybe in business as a trainer, gym owner or nutritionist.

Ideation (Messaging matrix)

Our next step was to create messaging matrix that defined high-level communication concept for each of the target groups across different communication channels. Together with Naked Labs team, we crafted a bunch of messages that sounded something like below:

  • “Give us 20 seconds and we’ll guide you back on track.”
  • “We track the small goals so you can focus on and reach the big goal.”
  • “You are not doing it alone!”
  • “Naked is a way to see exactly how your workouts affect your body.”
  • “We will add facts to your feelings.”

At the same time, to spark some inspiration for a website-specific content, we looked at other product websites that Naked Labs team loved. We came up with How Might We (HMW) questions and invited the Naked Labs team to generate ideas with us. Some of these questions included:

  • “HMW showcase our product on the new website?”
  • “HMW engage users as they scroll? ”
  • “HMW present our product tech specs?”
  • “HMW make user choose Naked editorial content?”
  • “HMW best assist our users when they have questions about our product?”

We drilled into a bunch of the best in class examples of product showcase, technical specifications, editorial content and support section pages and brainstormed on the specific ways certain features and content experiences could manifest in the new Naked website experience.

Through ideation & competitive analysis, we narrowed our question to: How might we create a website that combines a visually stunning brand and product showcase with rich lifestyle content experiences? We used this question to guide our further work.

Sitemap and content priority guides

Right after our immersion phase I created sitemap alongside content priority guides for the key pages and flows.

Considering size of this website, I suggested to ditch wireframes and use content priority guides instead that also worked as a content gathering document. I created the guides in a Google doc including each page with clearly defined elements awaiting for client to fill in. This format proved to work really well.

Visual direction

Based on insights gathered during our Research and Ideation phases, we were ready to prepare a themed visual vocabulary wall. Next up, we asked Naked Labs team to specify which visual references from this visual compass resonated best with their new brand vision.

The exercise resulted into a moodboard alongside Naked brand values that were extremely helpful for aligning with stakeholders and justifying the reasoning behind design decisions later on in the process.

Final Output

In a few months we redesigned and launched the Naked Labs website. We focused on making sure that the new website communicates a more premium brand and becomes a space that enables our client to share their product vision and connect with their audiences.
