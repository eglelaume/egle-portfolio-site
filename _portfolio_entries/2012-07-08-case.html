CASE project was set to re-platform The Economist’s e-commerce journey and, also, to create a new ‘manage my account’ dashboard from which a logged-in user could manage their subscriptions, bookmarks, comments and would be presented with different promotions.


I led visual design process and worked alongside a lead UX architect to create responsive solutions. I focused on visual brand integration and helped to improve user experience by rethinking all aspects of the user interaction. The image below gives you a taste for different user scenarios I had to focus on whilst working on the CASE.

Visual style

I had to deliver effective visual design and some improved UI solutions while articulating The Economist brand values. The tricky part was that the CASE project was happening alongside The Economist new website's development, meaning, I had to base my design rationale on the visual styles that were still fluctuating. As a result, some uncertainty was brought into the project's progression and I had to make sure to find ways to fulfil team's and stakeholders's expectations.


In order to achieve that, I put together a style guide that helped to document important decisions before starting work and ensured that globally distributed CASE team, the stakeholders and I were all on the same page from the outset. It also helped to introduce a level of visual consistency. You can see snippets from the style guide below.

From wires to prototypes

My day-to-day tasks involved translating low fidelity annotated wires into high fidelity click through prototypes so that marketing team could quickly review and test parts of the journeys. I mostly used Marvel and I found that “close to real” looking mocks really improved product owners feedback turnaround time.


One of the flows that required the most of stakeholders feedback was subscription renewals flow. You can see a snippet of different user state interactions I worked on below followed by just a few of many mocks I did for ‘manage my account’ dashboard.

Takeaway

The project taught me a lot about working as part of a globally distributed team of stakeholders. CASE is a huge scale project and is still ongoing.
