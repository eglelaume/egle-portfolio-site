Project overview

Global Trade Review (GTR) is a publisher and event organiser for the global trade, commodity and supply chain finance industries. I planned and led GTR digital rebrand that included design of the new responsive website, eNewsletters and confirmation emails. I was responsible for site map architecture followed by wireframes and mock-ups for multiple devices, also, at a later stage of the project, UI testing.

I played a key role in liaison with numerous teams including web developers, editorial and marketing. The latter involved face to face meetings, conference calls and a use of online collaboration tools such as Podio.

My role: graphic/web/UX designer.

Information architecture

A new responsive website for GTR was aimed to bring content of two different sites, news and events, together and make it accessible to users from their different devices.

Challenging but yet rewarding part of the project was that apart of visual design, I was heavily involved in analysing all different online personas who will be interacting with our site. Who are our users and what's the difference between a registered and a non-registered one? Will the new users be created in Wordpress or will the data be fetched from CRM?

All this brainstorming, resulted into me drawing up a site map together with some diagrams illustrating different user journeys and flows that where really helpful in communicating our ideas to developers. You can see snippets from my sketches below.

Wireframes

After the structure of the site was set I moved onto creating wireframes using an online UI prototyping tool called Moqups. This HTML5 app has a great collection of various UI stencils and allows importing images, meaning I could put together some high fidelity webpage wireframes for different devices.

After running the mock-ups by my company directors I moved on to creating some more detailed comps in Photoshop, some of which you can see below.

eNewsletters design

In line with the new website I also designed GTR eNewsletters. I had to design global eNewsletter for news along with the regional events eNewsletter. I aimed for a clutter free look with a featured news story or event on the top. Legible type and large elements are aimed to make user click through to our website.

Visual design

I wanted to keep the design on-brand and aimed for a clean and minimal design aesthetic. "Open Sans" font was used throughout the site because it has a great legibility and neutral, yet friendly appearance.

I used different font weights and colours to establish visual hierarchy. Regional content was colour coded and the highlight colour of mustard yellow was used throughout the site for call-to-action buttons.

Project overview

Premiere is a Lithuanian luxury jewellery retailer headquartered in Vilnius. I was tasked with designing a new responsive website for the retailer by also giving the brand a refresh. Website is still in development.

My role: graphic/web designer.

Designing visual style

Although Premiere branding had already been established, its visual style didn't convey the message of luxury. After talking to the client, I realised that Premiere stocks wide range of jewellery and my client wanted to appeal not only to those able to afford the most expensive products.

I decided that the brand needed to have more luxury upmarket aesthetic but not scare away those that could only afford cheaper jewellery. With that in mind, I came up with a bold colour palette dominated by dark royal blue and the highlight colours of cyan and red. Also, put together some playful yet minimal typography combinations.

Website structure

In line with a new visual style my client wanted a well-structured and clean looking website that emphasized beautiful products and gave customers a user friendly environment to shop online. After sketching out a few quick concepts and putting together some style tiles, I got straight into Photoshop.

My brief was to create a pre-checkout page that would prevent customer of abandoning the shopping cart. I made sure to include a large product image, related items, free shipping and product information and the ability to add the item to one's wish list if customer is not quite ready for it yet. Social media is a big part of Premiere's brand strategy so I decided to weave in social sharing options there too.
