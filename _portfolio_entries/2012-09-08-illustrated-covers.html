Global Trade Review (GTR) is a by-montly magazine that covers global trade, commodity, export and supply chain finance topics. I art directed and illustrated a few covers for the publication. Read about selected illustrations and their accompanying editorials below.

Hand-made cover

"The Great Remould"cover features a fictional world map illustrating the upcoming era in global trade. The biggest inspiration for the cover was David Horsey's caricature "The World According to Ronald Reagan", while the story's title made plasticine an obvious choice for the job.

I art directed the cover, led the photoshoot and did post-production work on it. The cover was made in collaboration with GTR's in-house design team.

Hand-drawn cover

For this cover I was tasked to illustrate a story that investigated questionable China’s commodities sector activities and a company nicknamed ‘the Qingdao Whale’.

With the concepts of 'searching' and 'hidden' in mind I started off with sketching a number of different cover versions. I then scanned and finalised the selected hand-drawn sketch in Illustrator. You can see my iterations and, also, the winning sketch versus finalised vector artwork below.

Flat style illustration cover

I was tasked to produce a cover illustration and a five page editorial for a progress report investigating the trade finance industry’s effort to become more environmentally and socially responsible. See the result I achieved in Illustrator below.

Vector-based cover

This cover illustration is an analogy between shared living space and the coexistence of old and new institutions in the world of trade.

The idea was to show that something very contemporary is going to be built on the top of something established. As a result, the illustration depicts a section of a Roman style country house featuring rather old-fashioned decor put against a fragment of a blueprint drawing for a contemporary house that accentuates simple proportions, open layouts and flexible living space.

The cover was made in collaboration with GTR's in-house design team.
